Portfolio Performance Evaluation

Measures of Return

There are several measures used to evaluate the performance of a portfolio:

  • Total Return: The sum of all returns from an investment, including capital gains and dividends, expressed as a percentage of the initial investment.
  • Annualized Return: The average annual return of an investment over a specified period.
  • Cumulative Return: The total return of an investment over a specified period.

Risk Adjusted Measures of Return

These measures take into account the risk taken to achieve a certain level of return:

  • Sharpe Ratio: Measures the risk-adjusted return of a portfolio, calculated as the portfolio's excess return over the risk-free rate divided by the portfolio's standard deviation.
  • Treynor Ratio: Similar to the Sharpe ratio, but uses beta as a measure of risk instead of standard deviation.

Market Timing

Market timing refers to the strategy of moving in and out of the market or switching between different assets based on predictions about future market movements.

Evaluation Criteria and Procedures

There are several criteria and procedures used to evaluate the performance of a portfolio:

  • Comparison with benchmark indices.
  • Peer group comparison.
  • Style analysis.
  • Performance attribution analysis.

Investment Policies of Individuals

Individual investors may have different investment policies based on their risk tolerance, investment goals, and time horizon.

Tax Saving Schemes in India

There are several tax saving schemes available in India, such as Equity Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS), Public Provident Fund (PPF), National Pension System (NPS), and Tax-saving Fixed Deposits.
