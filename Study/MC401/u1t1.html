Estimating Rate of Return

Rate of Return

Rate of return is the gain or loss on an investment over a specified period, expressed as a percentage of the investmentâ€™s cost.

Formula for Rate of Return

The formula to calculate rate of return is:

Rate of return = (Final value - Initial value) / Initial value * 100

Example

Suppose you invest Rs. 10,000 in a stock and after one year, the value of your investment is Rs. 11,500. To calculate the rate of return:

Rate of return = (11500 - 10000) / 10000 * 100 = 15%

Types of Rate of Return

  • Simple Rate of Return: It is the return on investment over a period, expressed as a percentage of the original investment. Formula: Simple Rate of Return = (Current Value - Initial Value) / Initial Value * 100
  • Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR): It is the average annual rate of return over a specified period, assuming that the investment has been compounding over that time. Formula: CAGR = (Final Value / Initial Value) ^ (1 / Number of Years) - 1

Types of Rate of Return (Hinglish)

Simple Rate of Return: Iska matlab hai seedhe tarikese kamai ya nuksan, jo investment ke original cost ke percentage mein hota hai.

Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR): Iska matlab hai ek nishchit avadhi ke liye average varshik rate of return, jisme assume kiya jata hai ki investment us samay se compound ho rahi hai.

Need for Estimating Rate of Return

Estimating the rate of return is crucial for investors to assess the performance of their investments and make informed decisions about future investments.

Nature/Features of Rate of Return

  • Relative measure expressed as a percentage.
  • Considers both gain or loss and the time period.

Advantages of Estimating Rate of Return

  • Helps in comparing the performance of different investments.
  • Provides a standardized measure to evaluate profitability.

Disadvantages of Estimating Rate of Return

  • Does not consider the risk associated with the investment.
  • May not account for external factors that could impact performance.

Conclusion

Estimating the rate of return is essential for investors to assess performance and make informed decisions, but should be used along with other measures.
