Portfolio Unique Risk

Definition

Portfolio unique risk, also known as unsystematic risk, is the risk that is specific to an individual stock or asset and can be diversified away by holding a diversified portfolio. It includes factors such as company-specific events, management changes, and industry risks.

Measurement of Portfolio Unique Risk

Portfolio unique risk can be measured using the standard deviation of a stock's returns. A higher standard deviation indicates higher unique risk, as it reflects the volatility of the stock's returns that is not related to market movements.

Diversification and Portfolio Unique Risk

Diversification is the key strategy for reducing portfolio unique risk. By holding a diversified portfolio that includes assets from different industries and geographic regions, investors can reduce the impact of individual stock events on the overall portfolio.

Benefits of Diversifying Portfolio Unique Risk

Diversifying portfolio unique risk has several benefits, including:

  • Reducing the impact of company-specific events on the overall portfolio.
  • Improving risk-adjusted returns by spreading risk across different assets.
  • Enhancing portfolio stability and reducing volatility.

Conclusion

Portfolio unique risk is an important consideration for investors, as it highlights the need for diversification to reduce the impact of individual stock events on the overall portfolio. By diversifying their portfolios, investors can reduce risk and improve their chances of achieving their investment goals.
