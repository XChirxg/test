Markowitz Risk-Return Optimization

Markowitz Portfolio Theory

Markowitz Portfolio Theory, developed by Harry Markowitz, is a framework for constructing portfolios that optimally balance risk and return.

Efficient Frontier

The efficient frontier is the set of optimal portfolios that offer the highest expected return for a given level of risk, or the lowest risk for a given level of return.

Formula for Markowitz Optimization

The formula to calculate the optimal portfolio weights for a given set of assets is:

Weight of Asset i = (Expected Return of Asset i - Risk-Free Rate) / (Standard Deviation of Portfolio)

Example

Suppose you have two assets, Asset 1 and Asset 2, with expected returns of 10% and 15% respectively, and standard deviations of 5% and 10% respectively. If the risk-free rate is 3%, the optimal weights would be:

Weight of Asset 1 = (10% - 3%) / Standard Deviation of Portfolio

Weight of Asset 2 = (15% - 3%) / Standard Deviation of Portfolio

Given that the standard deviation of the portfolio is calculated as in the previous example, the weights can be calculated accordingly.

Types of Portfolios

  • Minimum Variance Portfolio: Portfolio with the lowest possible variance.
  • Tangency Portfolio: Portfolio that lies on the efficient frontier and offers the highest Sharpe ratio.

Need for Markowitz Optimization

Markowitz optimization helps investors in constructing portfolios that offer the best possible trade-off between risk and return, based on their risk preferences.

Nature/Features of Markowitz Optimization

  • Focuses on diversification to reduce risk.
  • Uses historical data to estimate expected returns and standard deviations.

Advantages of Markowitz Optimization

  • Helps investors in constructing diversified portfolios.
  • Provides a systematic approach to balancing risk and return.

Disadvantages of Markowitz Optimization

  • Relies heavily on historical data, which may not always be reliable.
  • Assumes that the risk and return of assets can be accurately estimated, which may not always be the case.

Conclusion

Markowitz Risk-Return Optimization provides a valuable framework for investors to construct portfolios that balance risk and return, but it should be used along with other measures and considerations to make informed investment decisions.
