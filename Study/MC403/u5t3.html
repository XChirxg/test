Strategic Evaluation and Control - Overview and Nature

  • Strategic evaluation and control involve assessing the effectiveness of a company's strategy and making adjustments to ensure its successful implementation.
  • It is a continuous process that helps organizations stay on track toward their strategic goals and objectives.
  • Effective evaluation and control mechanisms are essential for monitoring progress, identifying issues, and making informed decisions.

Strategic Evaluation

  • Assessing the extent to which strategic goals and objectives are being achieved.
  • Evaluating the performance of the organization against key performance indicators (KPIs).
  • Identifying strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats (SWOT analysis) related to strategy implementation.

Strategic Control

  • Establishing mechanisms to monitor progress and performance against strategic goals.
  • Implementing corrective actions when performance deviates from the planned course.
  • Ensuring that resources are being used efficiently and effectively to support strategy implementation.

Requirements for Effective Evaluation

  • Clear and measurable strategic objectives.
  • Access to relevant and timely data for performance measurement.
  • Commitment from top management to support evaluation and control processes.
