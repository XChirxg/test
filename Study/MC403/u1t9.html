Strategy Formulation - Environmental and Organizational Appraisal

  • Strategy formulation is the process of developing strategies to achieve organizational objectives.
  • Environmental appraisal involves assessing the external factors that may impact the organization's performance and competitiveness.
  • Organizational appraisal involves evaluating the internal strengths and weaknesses of the organization, including its resources, capabilities, and core competencies.

Environmental Appraisal

  • Assessing the external environment to identify opportunities and threats.
  • Includes analyzing market trends, competitor behavior, and regulatory changes.
  • Tools and techniques: PESTLE analysis, Porter's Five Forces, SWOT analysis.

Organizational Appraisal

  • Evaluating the internal strengths and weaknesses of the organization.
  • Includes assessing resources, capabilities, and core competencies.
  • Tools and techniques: Resource-based view (RBV), value chain analysis, internal audits.

Integration of Appraisal

  • Aligning environmental and organizational appraisals to identify strategic options.
  • Identifying opportunities to leverage strengths and mitigate weaknesses.
  • Developing strategies that capitalize on external opportunities and internal capabilities.
