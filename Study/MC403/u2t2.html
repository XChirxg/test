Financial Analysis and Predicting Company Failure

  • Financial analysis involves assessing an organization's financial health and performance.
  • Predicting company failure requires analyzing financial statements and using financial ratios to identify warning signs of potential failure.
  • Financial analysis helps stakeholders make informed decisions about investing, lending, or doing business with a company.

Financial Analysis

  • Types of Financial Statements: Income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement.
  • Financial Ratios: Liquidity ratios, profitability ratios, solvency ratios, efficiency ratios.
  • Common Size Analysis: Expressing financial statement items as percentages of a base item.

Predicting Company Failure

  • Signs of Financial Distress: Declining profitability, liquidity problems, high debt levels.
  • Altman Z-Score: A formula for predicting bankruptcy based on financial ratios.
  • Other Predictive Models: Logit models, discriminant analysis.

Using Financial Analysis for Decision Making

  • Investment Decisions: Assessing the financial health of potential investments.
  • Lending Decisions: Evaluating the creditworthiness of borrowers.
  • Business Strategy: Identifying areas for improvement and strategic decision-making.
