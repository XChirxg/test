Business Policy and Genesis of Strategic Management and Business Policy

  • Business policy refers to the guidelines, principles, and rules of conduct formulated by an organization to achieve its long-term goals.
  • Strategic management and business policy are closely related disciplines that focus on the formulation and implementation of strategies to achieve organizational objectives.
  • The genesis of strategic management and business policy can be traced back to the early 20th century, with the emergence of formalized strategic planning processes in organizations.

Evolution of Strategic Management

  • Early Management Thought: Frederick Taylor's scientific management principles.
  • Classical Management Theories: Fayol's principles of management, Weber's bureaucratic theory.
  • Modern Management Theories: Drucker's management by objectives (MBO), Chandler's structure follows strategy.

Strategic Planning Process

  • Environmental Analysis: Assessing internal and external factors that may impact the organization.
  • Strategy Formulation: Developing strategies based on the analysis of the organization's strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats (SWOT).
  • Strategy Implementation: Executing the chosen strategies through action plans and organizational initiatives.
  • Strategy Evaluation: Assessing the effectiveness of strategies and making adjustments as needed.

Role of Business Policy

  • Provides a framework for decision-making and action within the organization.
  • Helps align individual and departmental goals with the overall objectives of the organization.
  • Ensures consistency and coherence in decision-making across the organization.
