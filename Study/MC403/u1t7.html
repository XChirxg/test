Strategic Leadership and Decision Making

  • Strategic leadership involves setting a clear vision for the organization, aligning resources, and motivating employees to achieve strategic objectives.
  • Effective strategic leaders are able to make informed decisions that drive organizational success.
  • Strategic decision-making requires a combination of analytical thinking, creativity, and the ability to anticipate and respond to change.

Characteristics of Strategic Leaders

  • Visionary: Able to articulate a compelling vision for the future.
  • Strategic Thinker: Able to see the big picture and anticipate future trends.
  • Decisive: Able to make tough decisions in complex and uncertain environments.
  • Collaborative: Able to build and lead high-performing teams.

Factors Influencing Strategic Decision Making

  • Internal Factors: Organizational culture, resources, and capabilities.
  • External Factors: Market conditions, competitive landscape, and regulatory environment.
  • Decision-Making Models: Rational, intuitive, and political models of decision making.

Challenges in Strategic Decision Making

  • Uncertainty: Limited information and unpredictable outcomes.
  • Complexity: Multiple stakeholders and interdependent variables.
  • Ambiguity: Lack of clarity and conflicting priorities.
