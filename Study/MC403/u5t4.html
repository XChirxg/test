Strategic Control and Operation Control

  • Strategic control involves monitoring the execution of a company's strategy and making adjustments to ensure its alignment with strategic goals.
  • Operation control, on the other hand, focuses on monitoring and managing day-to-day operations to ensure efficiency and effectiveness.
  • Both forms of control are essential for achieving strategic objectives and maintaining competitive advantage.

Strategic Control

  • Monitoring key performance indicators (KPIs) related to strategic objectives.
  • Assessing the external environment for changes that may impact the strategy.
  • Implementing corrective actions to realign strategy with changing conditions.

Operation Control

  • Monitoring daily operations to ensure they are in line with organizational goals and standards.
  • Identifying and addressing operational issues that may impact efficiency and effectiveness.
  • Implementing process improvements to enhance operational performance.

Integration of Strategic and Operation Control

  • Ensuring that operational decisions support strategic objectives.
  • Aligning operational processes with strategic goals to maximize efficiency and effectiveness.
  • Using feedback from operational performance to inform strategic decision-making.
