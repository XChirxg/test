Techniques of Strategic Evaluation and Control

  • Various techniques are used for strategic evaluation and control to assess the performance of a company's strategy and make informed decisions.
  • These techniques help in identifying strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats (SWOT analysis), and measuring progress against strategic goals.
  • Effective use of these techniques can enhance strategic planning and decision-making processes.

SWOT Analysis

  • Assessing the internal strengths and weaknesses of the organization and the external opportunities and threats it faces.
  • Helps in identifying strategic issues and developing strategies to address them.
  • Provides a framework for decision-making and strategy formulation.

Balanced Scorecard

  • Measuring organizational performance across four key perspectives: financial, customer, internal business processes, and learning and growth.
  • Provides a balanced view of organizational performance and helps in aligning strategic objectives with operational activities.
  • Facilitates communication and understanding of strategic objectives throughout the organization.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

  • Quantifiable measures used to evaluate the success of an organization in achieving its strategic objectives.
  • Help in monitoring progress, identifying areas for improvement, and making informed decisions.
  • Should be aligned with strategic goals and relevant to the organization's objectives.

Benchmarking

  • Comparing organizational performance against industry best practices or competitors.
  • Helps in identifying areas where the organization can improve and setting performance targets.
  • Provides insights into how other organizations achieve success and informs strategic decision-making.
