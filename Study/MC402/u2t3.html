Project Feasibility Study

Definition of Feasibility Study

A feasibility study is an assessment of the practicality and potential success of a proposed project. It evaluates various aspects such as economic, technical, legal, and scheduling feasibility.

Objectives of Feasibility Study

The main objectives of a feasibility study are to determine whether a project is viable, identify potential risks and challenges, and provide a basis for decision-making regarding the project.

Components of Feasibility Study

A feasibility study typically includes an analysis of market feasibility, technical feasibility, financial feasibility, organizational feasibility, and legal feasibility.

Methods for Conducting Feasibility Study

There are various methods for conducting a feasibility study, including market surveys, technical assessments, financial projections, and risk analysis. These methods help assess the viability of the project.

Importance of Feasibility Study

Conducting a feasibility study is important to minimize risks and maximize the chances of project success. It provides stakeholders with valuable information to make informed decisions about the project.

Example

For example, a real estate developer may conduct a feasibility study before starting a new housing project. The study would assess factors such as market demand, construction costs, regulatory requirements, and potential returns on investment.
