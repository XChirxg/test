Cost of Project and Means of Financing

Cost of Project

The cost of a project includes all the expenses incurred in setting up and running the project. It includes the cost of land, buildings, machinery, equipment, labor, materials, and overheads.

Components of Project Cost

  • Fixed Capital: This includes the cost of land, buildings, and machinery that are not expected to change over the life of the project.
  • Working Capital: This includes the cost of raw materials, labor, and other expenses that are required for day-to-day operations of the project.
  • Preliminary and Preoperative Expenses: These include expenses such as feasibility studies, project reports, legal expenses, and other pre-project expenses.
  • Contingency Fund: This is a fund set aside to cover unexpected expenses or cost overruns during the project.

Means of Financing

There are various means through which a project can be financed, including:

  • Equity: This involves raising capital by issuing shares to investors. It represents ownership in the company and does not need to be repaid.
  • Debt: This involves borrowing money from lenders, such as banks or financial institutions, and repaying it with interest over time.
  • Internal Sources: This includes funds generated from the company's operations, such as profits reinvested in the business.
  • Government Assistance: This includes grants, subsidies, or tax incentives provided by the government to support the project.

Factors Influencing Financing Decision

  • Cost of Capital
  • Risk Profile of the Project
  • Availability of Funds
  • Term of Financing
  • Tax Considerations
