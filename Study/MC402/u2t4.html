Appraisal Criteria and Process

Definition of Appraisal

Appraisal is the process of assessing the value or worth of a project or investment opportunity. It involves evaluating various criteria to determine the feasibility and potential return on investment of the project.

Appraisal Criteria

Appraisal criteria are the standards or benchmarks used to evaluate the feasibility and potential success of a project. These criteria typically include financial viability, market demand, technical feasibility, and organizational capability.

Appraisal Process

The appraisal process involves several steps, including identifying the need for the appraisal, defining the appraisal objectives, gathering and analyzing relevant data, evaluating alternatives, and making recommendations based on the findings.

Methods of Appraisal

There are various methods of project appraisal, including payback period, net present value (NPV), internal rate of return (IRR), and profitability index. These methods help assess the financial viability and potential return on investment of the project.

Importance of Appraisal

Appraisal is important as it helps organizations make informed decisions about whether to pursue a project or investment opportunity. It provides a basis for allocating resources and mitigating risks.

Example

For example, a manufacturing company may conduct an appraisal of a new production facility. The appraisal would assess factors such as the cost of construction, expected revenue from the facility, and the payback period.
