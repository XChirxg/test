L & M and UNIDO Approach

L & M Approach

The L & M approach to social cost-benefit analysis (SCBA) is based on the work of economists J. R. Hicks and R. G. D. Allen. It focuses on the measurement of social welfare changes resulting from a project or policy.

Key Concepts of L & M Approach

  • Compensating Variation (CV): The amount of money that would need to be given to individuals to compensate them for the welfare loss caused by a project or policy.
  • Equivalent Variation (EV): The amount of money that individuals would be willing to pay to receive the welfare gain from a project or policy.
  • Consumer Surplus: The difference between what consumers are willing to pay for a good or service and what they actually pay.
  • Producer Surplus: The difference between the price producers receive for a good or service and the minimum price they are willing to accept.

UNIDO Approach

The United Nations Industrial Development Organization (UNIDO) approach to SCBA emphasizes the economic and social impacts of industrial development projects in developing countries.

Key Concepts of UNIDO Approach

  • Economic Rate of Return (ERR): The rate of return on investment that is necessary to make a project economically viable.
  • Financial Rate of Return (FRR): The rate of return on investment that is necessary to cover all costs and provide a return to investors.
  • Net Present Value (NPV): The difference between the present value of benefits and the present value of costs over the life of a project.
  • Internal Rate of Return (IRR): The discount rate that makes the NPV of a project equal to zero.
