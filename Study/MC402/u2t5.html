Methods of Appraisal under Certainty, Uncertainty, and Risk

Appraisal under Certainty

  • Under certainty, the outcome of a project is known with certainty.
  • Methods such as payback period, accounting rate of return (ARR), and net present value (NPV) are commonly used for appraisal.
  • These methods help determine the financial viability of a project when the outcome is certain.

Appraisal under Uncertainty

  • Under uncertainty, the outcome of a project is not known with certainty.
  • Methods such as decision tree analysis and sensitivity analysis are used to assess the project's viability under different scenarios.
  • These methods help account for the uncertainty in project outcomes and make informed decisions.

Appraisal under Risk

  • Under risk, the probability of different outcomes is known.
  • Methods such as expected monetary value (EMV) analysis and Monte Carlo simulation are used to assess the project's viability based on the probabilities of different outcomes.
  • These methods help account for the risk in project outcomes and make risk-adjusted decisions.

Comparison of Methods

  • Each method has its strengths and weaknesses, and the choice of method depends on the level of certainty, uncertainty, or risk associated with the project.
  • It is important to use multiple methods and sensitivity analysis to assess the robustness of the appraisal results.
